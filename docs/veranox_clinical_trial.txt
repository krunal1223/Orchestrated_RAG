CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Study ID: NCT-2024-VERANOX-007
Title: Phase III Efficacy and Safety of Veranox-40 in Treatment-Resistant Major Depressive Disorder
Sponsor: Meridian Pharmaceuticals Inc.
Principal Investigator: Dr. Sophia Lindqvist, MD PhD
Institution: Karolinska Institute, Stockholm, Sweden
Report Date: October 15, 2024

=======================================================
ABSTRACT
=======================================================

Background: Treatment-resistant major depressive disorder (TR-MDD) affects approximately 30% of MDD patients who fail to respond to at least two adequate antidepressant trials. Veranox-40 is a novel dual-mechanism agent acting as both a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) and a kappa-opioid receptor antagonist (KORA).

Methods: 847 adult patients with TR-MDD were enrolled across 23 clinical sites in 8 countries. Patients were randomized 1:1 to receive Veranox-40 (40mg daily oral) or placebo for 16 weeks. Primary endpoint: change from baseline in Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (HDRS-17) at week 16. Secondary endpoints included remission rate (HDRS-17 ≤7), response rate (≥50% reduction in HDRS-17), and Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) scores.

Results: Veranox-40 demonstrated statistically significant superiority over placebo on all primary and secondary endpoints. Mean HDRS-17 reduction: 14.3 points (Veranox-40) vs 7.1 points (placebo), p<0.0001. Remission rate: 38.4% vs 16.2%, NNT=4.5. Response rate: 54.7% vs 28.3%.

Conclusion: Veranox-40 represents a clinically meaningful advance for TR-MDD with an acceptable safety profile. Meridian Pharmaceuticals plans to submit a New Drug Application (NDA) to the FDA by Q1 2025.

=======================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
=======================================================

Major depressive disorder (MDD) affects approximately 280 million people globally (WHO, 2023). Of these, roughly 84 million suffer from treatment-resistant forms where standard therapies fail. Current options for TR-MDD are limited: electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS), and esketamine (Spravato) are the primary approved alternatives, each with significant limitations in tolerability, access, or durability.

Veranox-40 was developed by Meridian Pharmaceuticals over a 9-year program beginning in 2015. The compound (chemical name: 4-[(2R)-2-methylpiperazin-1-yl]-2-(trifluoromethyl)benzamide hydrochloride) emerged from a computational drug discovery platform that identified the kappa-opioid pathway as a key modulator of anhedonia, a core symptom of depression not adequately addressed by SSRIs alone.

Phase I studies (2019-2020, n=64): Established safety and pharmacokinetics. Half-life: 18.4 hours. Bioavailability: 73%. No QTc prolongation observed.
Phase II studies (2021-2022, n=214): Dose-finding. 40mg daily identified as optimal dose (superior to 20mg and 80mg on efficacy/tolerability balance).

=======================================================
2. METHODS
=======================================================

2.1 Patient Population
Inclusion criteria:
- Age 22-68 years
- Diagnosis of MDD (DSM-5) with current episode >6 months duration
- HDRS-17 score ≥20 at screening
- Failed ≥2 adequate antidepressant trials (adequate = ≥6 weeks at recommended dose)
- Stable on no antidepressants for ≥4 weeks prior to enrollment (washout period)

Exclusion criteria:
- Bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, or active substance use disorder
- Active suicidal ideation with plan or intent
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B or C)
- Concurrent use of MAOIs, strong CYP3A4 inhibitors, or opioid medications

2.2 Randomization and Blinding
Patients were randomized using a central computerized system stratified by site and baseline HDRS-17 severity (moderate: 20-24; severe: ≥25). Both patients and investigators were blinded to treatment assignment. Matching placebo capsules were used. Treatment allocation was concealed using sealed envelopes opened only at study completion.

2.3 Statistical Analysis
Primary analysis: ANCOVA with treatment group as factor, baseline HDRS-17 and stratification factors as covariates. Modified intent-to-treat (mITT) population defined as all randomized patients who received ≥1 dose and had ≥1 post-baseline assessment. Missing data handled by multiple imputation. Pre-specified sensitivity analyses confirmed primary results. Alpha set at 0.05 (two-sided). Statistical power: 90% to detect minimum clinically important difference of 3 points on HDRS-17.

=======================================================
3. RESULTS
=======================================================

3.1 Patient Disposition
Enrolled: 847 patients (423 Veranox-40; 424 placebo)
Completed 16 weeks: 371 (87.7%) Veranox-40; 358 (84.4%) placebo
Discontinued: 52 Veranox-40 (12.3%); 66 placebo (15.6%)
Reasons for discontinuation (Veranox-40 / placebo):
- Adverse events: 28 / 14
- Withdrawal of consent: 16 / 31
- Lost to follow-up: 8 / 21

3.2 Primary Endpoint
Mean baseline HDRS-17: 26.8 (Veranox-40), 27.1 (placebo) — well-balanced.
Mean change from baseline at Week 16 (mITT, n=831):
- Veranox-40: -14.3 points (SD 6.1)
- Placebo: -7.1 points (SD 5.8)
- Difference: -7.2 points (95% CI: -8.1 to -6.3), p<0.0001
- Effect size (Cohen's d): 0.96 — considered large

3.3 Secondary Endpoints
Remission rate (HDRS-17 ≤7) at Week 16:
- Veranox-40: 38.4% (162/422)
- Placebo: 16.2% (68/420)
- p<0.0001; NNT = 4.5

Response rate (≥50% reduction HDRS-17):
- Veranox-40: 54.7% (231/422)
- Placebo: 28.3% (119/420)
- p<0.0001; Odds ratio: 3.07 (95% CI: 2.28–4.13)

PHQ-9 change from baseline:
- Veranox-40: -9.4 points
- Placebo: -4.8 points
- Difference: -4.6 (p<0.0001)

Onset of action: Statistically significant separation from placebo observed at Week 2 (p=0.018), earlier than most existing antidepressants.

3.4 Safety and Tolerability
Most common adverse events (≥5% in either group):
- Nausea: 31.2% (Veranox-40) vs 14.1% (placebo) — most resolved by Week 4
- Headache: 22.7% vs 19.3%
- Insomnia: 18.4% vs 15.6%
- Dry mouth: 17.9% vs 8.2%
- Dizziness: 14.3% vs 9.7%
- Decreased libido: 12.1% vs 6.8%

Serious adverse events (SAEs):
- Veranox-40: 11 SAEs in 9 patients (2.1%)
- Placebo: 8 SAEs in 7 patients (1.6%)
- No statistically significant difference (p=0.62)
- No deaths occurred in either group during the study

Liver function: 3 patients on Veranox-40 showed transient ALT elevation >3x ULN; all resolved with discontinuation. No cases of drug-induced liver injury (DILI) meeting Hy's Law criteria.

Weight: Mean weight change: +1.4 kg (Veranox-40) vs -0.3 kg (placebo) at 16 weeks.

=======================================================
4. DISCUSSION
=======================================================

Veranox-40 achieved a Cohen's d of 0.96, substantially larger than typical antidepressant effect sizes (0.3-0.5) in TR-MDD populations. The NNT of 4.5 for remission compares favorably to esketamine (NNT ~5-7 in comparable populations).

The early onset at Week 2 is clinically significant — rapid response in depression reduces suicide risk during the acute phase and improves patient retention in treatment.

The kappa-opioid antagonism component specifically targets anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure), a symptom notoriously resistant to SSRIs alone. This dual mechanism likely explains the superior efficacy.

Limitations:
- 16-week trial does not address long-term efficacy and durability
- Nausea rate (31.2%) may limit tolerability in real-world practice
- Study excluded patients >68 years; elderly data needed
- The weight gain signal (+1.4 kg) warrants monitoring in longer trials
